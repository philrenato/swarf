concordance every named thing in swarf, one line each

operations

rough
the first pass. clears bulk material in horizontal layers above the part. fast, deep, sloppy by design — you finish later. typical step-down: 30–80% of tool diameter; typical stepover: 40–60%.
contour
smooth surface pass after rough. the cutter crawls across the X or Y axis at a fine stepover, riding the part's curves. typical stepover: 5–15% of tool diameter on finishing.
outline
cuts the part free from the stock by following its silhouette. usually the last cutting op. leave material with tabs so the part doesn't fly out.
pocket
clears flat-bottomed cavities — trays, countersinks, recesses. selection-driven; pick the floor face and swarf walls down.

parameters

stepover
how far the tool moves sideways between passes. percentage of tool diameter. larger = faster but rougher; smaller = smoother but slower. roughing 40–60%, finishing 5–15%.
step-down (depth of cut)
how deep the tool plunges per layer. limited by tool reach and material. wood/plastic up to 1× tool diameter; aluminum 0.25–0.5×; steel 0.1–0.25×.
feed rate
how fast the tool moves through material, in mm/min. material + tool + step-down all affect this. when in doubt: slower is safer.
plunge rate
how fast the tool drives down into material. usually 30–50% of feed rate. too fast and you snap end-mills.
spindle speed
RPM of the cutter. softer material + bigger tool = lower RPM. wood 12k–24k; aluminum 10k–18k; steel 4k–10k.
ease down
ramp into the cut at an angle instead of plunging straight down. easier on the tool, especially for end-mills with no center cut. on by default in swarf.
depth first
finish each Z layer top-to-bottom in one region before moving to the next. opposite is layer-first (sweep all regions per layer).
tab
a small uncut bridge of stock that holds the part in place during the outline cut, so it doesn't shift or fly out. break or sand off after.
stock
the raw block of material on the machine bed. defaults to part-bbox in swarf student mode. expert mode lets you size it independently.
z top / z bottom
upper and lower Z bounds for the carving. defines the slab swarf will work inside.
z clearance
how high above stock the tool retracts during rapids. high enough to clear clamps; low enough not to waste time.

tools

end-mill
flat-bottomed cutter, the workhorse. diameter and flute count vary; fewer flutes = better chip clearance for soft material.
ball-end
spherical tip. for 3D contour finishing on curved surfaces. leaves a cusp pattern proportional to stepover.
v-bit
conical cutter for engraving and chamfering. depth controls the line width.
drill
plunges straight down to make holes. paired with the drill operation (expert mode).

machines

Langmuir MR-1
benchtop CNC mill, 30×17×16" envelope, BT30 spindle. swarf's default device.
ShopBot Basic
4×8' router, ER25 spindle. for sheet-goods carving and signage.

materials (starting defaults)

hardwood
oak, maple, walnut, cherry — sharp HSS leaves a clean edge. dust mask. feed, plunge, spindle, step-down per tool diameter live in swarf-materials.json.
foam
rigid PU, EPS, blue/pink insulation. fastest cuts in the library; main risk is tool deflection on long flutes.
aluminum 6061
chips should curl, not powder. keep RPM as low as the spindle allows. a mist of WD-40 or alcohol helps.
polycarbonate
Lexan and similar. gummy — melts if you feed too slow. single-flute O-flutes are ideal; 2-flute HSS works if chips eject.
mild steel
occasional only. HSS on prosumer CNC is slow and punishing. small stepdowns, lots of lubricant, listen for chatter and back off.

simulation

chips
during Simulate, the cutter throws particles along its spin tangent with a kick in the feed direction. four flavors mix — long helical curls, triangular flakes, tight c-coils, and fine grit. gravity pulls them down; they settle on the build platform and stay there. import a new part to clear the floor.
phase aura
the whole scene gently tints to the current workflow step — neutral when idle, warm while toolpathing, cool while preview slices, hot mill-red while simulating, achromatic after gcode export. touch anything and the color returns.
phase LED
a small dot next to the renato.design watermark. dim = idle, amber = toolpaths, gold = preview, glowing mill-red = simulate, gray = exported. always on, always quiet.
sky
procedural two-layer mackerel sky with slow drift and camera-orbit parallax. when swarf is thinking (slicing, preparing, simulating) the clouds speed up. sunset palette behind it — yellow horizon, red band, violet, indigo zenith.
